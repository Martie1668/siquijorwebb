Siquijor — 2025

Siquijor is a beautiful island in the Philippines known for its stunning beaches, waterfalls, and mystic traditions. Often called the "Island of Fire," it has a rich folklore of healing and witchcraft, but today, it's a peaceful paradise perfect for adventure and relaxation.

Contabon Cave

Central Siquijor

Cantabon Cave is a limestone cave, a type of k arst formation. Karst landscapes are formed by the dissolution of soluble rocks, s uch as limestone, dolomite, and gypsum. This process leads to the creation of caves, sinkholes, and other unique landforms. The specific geological processes that led to the formation of Cantabon Cave would involve the gradual dissolution of limestone by acidic water, seeping through cracks and fissures over thousands of years.

Cambugahay Falls

Lazi, Siquijor

The biggest and most beautiful waterfall in Siquijor Cambugahay Falls is a three-tiered gem of cool, clear, cascading water located in Lazi town. A jewel surrounded by lush forest, Siquijor Cambugahay Falls offers a myriad of activities aside from soothing your weary soul in its refreshing waters.

Activities to do in Siquijor

Siquijor, known as the “Mystical Island,” offers a mix of adventure, history, and relaxation. Visit Cambugahay Falls for a refreshing swim and thrilling rope swings.

Explore the Old Enchanted Balete Tree, where you can dip your feet in a natural fish spa. Enjoy the white sands and crystal-clear waters at Salagdoong Beach, famous for its cliff diving spots.

Discover the island’s rich history at St. Francis of Assisi Church and Lazi Church & Convent, both Spanish-era landmarks. Go spelunking in Cantabon Cave, a hidden gem filled with stalactites and underground streams. Take a scenic drive around the island on a rented motorbike to enjoy breathtaking coastal views.

Witness a magical sunset at Paliton Beach, often compared to Boracay for its beauty. Experience local mysticism by visiting traditional healers who practice ancient healing rituals. Finally, cap off your trip with a relaxing beachfront dinner, enjoying fresh seafood while listening to the waves.

Why you choose Siquijor

Siquijor is the perfect destination for travelers seeking a unique blend of adventure, history, and relaxation. Its pristine beaches and turquoise waters make it an ideal spot for swimming, snorkeling, and diving. The island’s mystical charm, with legends of healers and ancient rituals, adds a fascinating cultural touch. Unlike crowded tourist spots, Siquijor offers a peaceful and laid-back atmosphere, perfect for unwinding. Nature lovers will enjoy its majestic waterfalls, hidden caves, and lush f orests. The island is also rich in Spanish-era heritage, with well-preserved churches and convents.

Siquijor’s friendly locals and affordable travel costs make it a budget-friendly paradise. Adventure seekers can explore the island on a motorbike, discovering hidden gems along the scenic coastal roads. With its breathtaking sunsets and serene ambiance, Siquijor is an ideal getaway for couples, families, and solo travelers. Whether you’re lo oking for relaxation, adventure, or a touch of mystery, Siquijor has something special to offer.

Delicacies in Siquijor

Siquijor is home to a variety of delicious local delicacies. One of the most popular treats is calamay, a sweet, sticky dessert made from coconut milk, brown sugar, and glutinous rice. For seafood lovers, the island offers an abundance of fresh catches, including grilled fish, shrimp, and shellfish. The famous Siquijor-style BBQ features tender pork and chicken skewers, often paired with a tangy dipping sauce. Another must-try is lobster, which is served fresh and cooked to perfection. Lamia, a sweet delicacy made from sugarcane, is another island favorite. Buko, or fresh coconut water, is widely available, offering a refreshing drink on hot days.

For those looking for a strong local spirit, Siquijor lambanog is a popular coconut-based liquor. The island also has a variety of fruit shakes made from mangoes, pineapples, and bananas, perfect for a tropical refreshment. These delicacies, combined with the island’s laid-back atmosphere, offer a unique culinary experience for visitors.
